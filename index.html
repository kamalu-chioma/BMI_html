Why BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple yet effective tool used globally to assess an individual's body weight relative to their height. It helps categorize weight into different health-related categories, such as Underweight, Healthy Weight, Overweight, and Obesity.

While BMI isn't a direct measure of body fat, it serves as a quick indicator of potential health risks like cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, and more. Tracking your BMI over time can also help monitor progress towards fitness and health goals.

Did you know? The concept of BMI was first introduced in the 1830s by Belgian mathematician Adolphe Quetelet. Today, it's a widely recognized standard in medical and fitness fields for evaluating overall health.
